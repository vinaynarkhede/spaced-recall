QUESTION: WHAT are the three private IP ranges (RFC 1918)? WHY do they exist and when to use each?
ANSWER: <b>THE THREE RANGES:</b><br><br>1. <b>10.0.0.0/8</b> (Class A)<br>• Size: 16,777,216 addresses<br>• Use: Large enterprises, data centers<br>• Example: 10.0.1.50<br><br>2. <b>172.16.0.0/12</b> (Class B)<br>• Size: 1,048,576 addresses<br>• Range: 172.16.x.x to 172.31.x.x<br>• Use: Docker, WSL2, VPNs<br>• Example: 172.17.0.2 (Docker container)<br><br>3. <b>192.168.0.0/16</b> (Class C)<br>• Size: 65,536 addresses<br>• Use: Home networks, small offices<br>• Example: 192.168.1.1 (your router)<br><br><b>WHY THEY EXIST (RFC 1918, 1996):</b><br>• Free to use (no registration)<br>• Internet routers won't route them (security)<br>• Reusable (every home uses 192.168.x.x)<br>• NAT translates to public IP when needed<br><br><b>KEY INSIGHT:</b> These ranges provide isolation AND allow infinite reuse. Without them, we'd have run out of IPv4 addresses decades ago.
